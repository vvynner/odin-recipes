Pad Thai

Descriptions

is a stir-fried rice noodle dish commonly served as a street food in Thailand as part of the country's cuisine. It is typically made with rice noodles, shrimp, peanuts, a scrambled egg, and bean sprouts, among other vegetables. The ingredients are fried in a wok.

Breif Ingredients

  • 1 (12 ounce) package rice noodles
  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast halves, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil
  • 4 eggs
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 lemon, cut into wedges
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce
  • ¼ cup crushed peanuts
  • 1 tablespoon of PadThai Paste

Steps

  1. Soak rice noodles in cold water until soft, 30 to 50 minutes. Drain and set aside.
  2. Heat butter in a wok; add chicken and sauté until browned. Remove chicken and set aside.
  3. Heat oil in the wok over medium-high heat. Crack eggs into hot oil and cook until firm. Stir in chicken and cook for 5 minutes. Add softened noodles, sugar, fish sauce, vinegar, and red pepper; mix well until noodles are tender. Adjust seasonings to taste. Stir in bean sprouts and cook for 3 minutes.
  4. Serve topped with green onions, crushed peanuts, and a wedge of lemon.
